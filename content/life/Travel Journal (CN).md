# Travel Journal

> [!quote] Saint Augustine
> "The world is a book, and those who do not travel read only one page."

## 2024 Trip: Kyoto, Japan

> [!info] Trip Details
> - **Dates**: March 20-30, 2024
> - **Duration**: 10 days
> - **Budget**: ¥200,000 (~$1,300 USD)
> - **Highlights**: Temples, cherry blossoms, food

### Day 1: Arrival

Landed at Kansai International Airport. The train to Kyoto was incredibly efficient — a stark contrast to the transportation chaos described in some [[Smart Home Automation|smart city]] discussions.

> [!tip] Travel Tip
> Get an ICOCA card for public transport. It works on trains, buses, and even convenience stores.

### Day 3: Fushimi Inari Shrine

> [!note] Fushimi Inari
> Famous for its thousands of vermillion torii gates. The full hike takes about 2-3 hours.

The hike was both physically demanding and spiritually moving. I reflected on the concepts from [[Book Notes - Thinking Fast and Slow]] about how environment shapes our experience.

> [!warning] Currency Tip
> Japan is still cash-heavy. Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs — they accept foreign cards.

## Food Highlights

> [!example] Must-Try Foods
> 1. **Ramen** at Ichiran — private booth dining
> 2. **Sushi** at the Nishiki Market
> 3. **Matcha** desserts in Uji
> 4. **Wagyu beef** — worth the splurge
> 5. **Onigiri** from convenience stores — surprisingly good

## Photography Notes

> [!tip] Camera Settings for Temples
> - Aperture: f/8 for sharp details
> - ISO: 200-400 in shaded areas
> - White balance: Cloudy for warm tones
